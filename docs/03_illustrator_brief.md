# The Casefilers — Illustrator Brief

*For commissioning the four filer portraits. Version 1.0.*

---

## Project summary

The Casefilers is a four-filer underground public-source intelligence unit. The product needs four illustrated portraits, one per filer, to anchor the unit's identity across the application chrome and the marketing site.

These are not decorative illustrations. They are the visual representation of the four people who do the work. The portraits will appear in the sidebar, on filer profile pages, in the activation transition, in the case file, and on the marketing site. They will be the most-seen single asset in the product.

Each portrait must hold up at 24px (a sidebar badge) and at 200px (a profile page). The compositions must be *immediately distinguishable* from one another at thumbnail size.

---

## Style — overall

**Aesthetic:** noir / mid-century intelligence agency. Saul Bass meets Le Carré. The visual world of *Slow Horses*, *Tinker Tailor Soldier Spy*, *The Americans* opening titles.

**Treatment:** Heavy silhouettes. Faces obscured or simplified. Dramatic single-light-source feel. Flat illustration.

**Format:** Black silhouette on transparent background. The product places these portraits on cream paper panels (`#f0ebde`), so the negative space inside the figure is the *paper showing through*.

**Style requirements:**

- Flat illustration. No gradients, no rendering, no photographic detail.
- No color. Black on transparent, that's it. The product applies color via the panel behind.
- Faces deliberately obscured — by hat brim, by shadow, by hair, by the document they hold. We never see eyes clearly.
- Each figure cropped at the chest, shoulder, or desk edge.
- All four must share line weight, palette (just black), and visual register.

---

## The critical rule — distinguishable at 24px

The single most important requirement: each silhouette must be *immediately distinguishable* from the other three at thumbnail size. If you blurred them down to 24×24 pixels and saw only the outlines, you should still be able to tell who's who.

**The way to achieve this:** each filer has a different *dominant shape*. Do not give all four similar coat-and-hat silhouettes — that's the trap.

| Filer | Dominant shape at 24px |
|---|---|
| Infiltrator | Wide-brim hat with asymmetric collar |
| Ghost | Full figure walking away with folder under arm |
| Nexus | Leaning seated figure with desk and hands forward |
| Oracle | Seated figure with document held up at reading height |

These shapes are *non-negotiable* for the silhouette identity to work.

## The four filers — full specifications

### 01 — Infiltrator (woman)

**Role:** Finds what search misses. Works the public web aggressively. The filer who *goes in*.

**Composition:** Bust-up portrait, head-and-shoulders. Facing forward, head turned slightly. Confident, alert.

**Signature shape:** Wide-brimmed black hat angled low over her eyes. Sharp jawline visible below the brim. Dark lipstick — the single high-contrast feature in an otherwise dark face. Hair pulled into a low bun visible behind the brim.

**Wardrobe:** Dark high-collared coat or trench. Lapels popped sharply, asymmetric — one side higher than the other to give her forward-motion energy even when static.

**Carries:** Nothing. Her hands are empty. *She's about to go out and get material.*

**Mood:** *The one who knocks.* She is the most "present" of the four — facing the viewer, alert, ready.

**Crop:** Square 1:1 for product. Marketing crop can extend down to mid-torso.

---

### 02 — Ghost (man)

**Role:** Recovers what disappeared. Wayback Machine, defunct forums, leaked archives. Returns with material others have let go.

**Composition:** **Full figure, three-quarter turn away from the viewer, mid-stride.** This is the single most important compositional move in the set. Ghost is the only filer who isn't facing the camera. He is walking out of frame.

**Signature shape:** A flat cap or soft trilby (not a fedora). Long dark overcoat from behind, collar up, back slightly hunched, the hem of the coat catching air as he moves. The hat in profile.

**Carries:** A manila folder tucked under one arm. **The folder is the single most narratively dense element in the entire set of four portraits.** It must be readable at every size — make it slightly oversized relative to the figure in the small versions if needed. The folder's lighter color against the dark coat is the highest-contrast detail in his silhouette and the first thing the viewer's eye lands on.

**Mood:** *Already leaving.* He doesn't stay in the room. He arrives with material and goes back out.

**Crop:** This is the only filer where the natural crop is *tall* (full figure). For product use, frame him in a 1:1 square with empty space on the right where he's walking *into*. Do not center him — bias him toward the left edge of the frame.

---

### 03 — Nexus (man)

**Role:** Connects the record. Pins entities, builds timelines, marks contradictions.

**Composition:** Seated half-figure, cropped at desk edge. Leaning forward over the desk. Bust-up composition with the desk forming a strong horizontal base.

**Signature shape:** Both hands visible — one holding a pen, the other steadying or flat on a document. **The hands are part of his identity.** He is the only filer whose hands appear in his portrait.

**Wardrobe:** Rolled shirtsleeves, loose tie, no jacket. Slightly disheveled hair. He looks like he has been at this for hours.

**Carries:** A pen, writing on a document. The document is on the desk, partially visible — lines of text suggested but not rendered.

**Face:** Obscured by the angle of his head looking down at the desk. We see the top of his head and the line of his shoulders, but not his eyes.

**Mood:** *Working through the night.* Focused, immobile, internal.

**Crop:** Square 1:1 with the desk forming the bottom edge of the frame.

---

### 04 — Oracle (woman)

**Role:** Reads the corpus. Writes the finding. Signs every file.

**Composition:** Seated upright at her desk, holding a single document at reading height in both hands. Facing slightly to the side. The document is between her face and the viewer.

**Signature shape:** **The document itself.** A single page or thin sheaf held up vertically in front of her face. The page is the brightest, sharpest element in the portrait — its rectangular edges are crisp, the negative space of the paper is what the viewer notices first. The document partially obscures her face.

**Hands:** Both visible, holding the document — slender, careful, deliberate. She handles paper like it's evidence.

**Wardrobe:** Dark blouse or suit jacket. Long hair falling past her shoulder. A single earring just catching light at her ear (small but present — it humanizes her and adds a specific identifying feature).

**Face:** Partially obscured by the document. Only half her face is visible, and that half is partly in shadow.

**Mood:** *Quiet, exact, unsentimental.* She is the most still of the four. She is the one who stays in the room.

**Crop:** Square 1:1, with the desk forming the bottom edge of the frame.

## How to test the set

1. **Print all four at 24×24 pixels.** Squint. Can you identify each filer just from the silhouette?
   - The one facing forward in a wide hat (Infiltrator)
   - The one walking away with a folder (Ghost)
   - The leaning figure with hands on a desk (Nexus)
   - The figure with a document held up at face height (Oracle)

   If any two read the same at 24px, the composition is not yet distinct enough.

2. **Print all four side by side at full size.** Do they feel like four people from the same unit? Same line weight, same illustration style, same level of detail? Or do they feel like four different illustrators?

3. **Place each one on a cream paper background (`#f0ebde`).** Can you still see Ghost's folder? Oracle's document? Nexus's pen and paper? The negative-space details must read clearly against cream, because that's where they live in production.

## Hand-paper economy — the silent narrative

This is worth understanding because it informs every decision.

The four portraits together encode the unit's entire workflow through what each filer is holding:

- Infiltrator carries nothing (about to go out)
- Ghost returns with a folder (recovered material)
- Nexus writes on a document (entering items into the file)
- Oracle reads a document (delivering the finding)

This is the entire pipeline of the unit, visible in the portraits before a word of explanation. *The work moves between the four of them, paper to paper, until it reaches Oracle.*

This is a non-negotiable feature of the set. Every filer except Infiltrator must clearly hold paper. Infiltrator must clearly *not* hold paper.

---

## Do not include

- Tech imagery: no screens, no laptops, no headphones, no cables
- Weapons or tactical gear
- "Hacker" tropes: no hoodies, no green code, no balaclavas, no masks
- AI / robot imagery of any kind
- Smiles or visible expressions
- Modern clothing — keep it timeless mid-century, not 2020s casual
- Color (except in the inverted dark-mode version, which stays monochrome)
- Decorative details — keep the silhouettes clean and graphic

---

## Deliverables — what we need from the illustrator

For each of the four filers:

1. **Master file** — vector (.svg, .ai, or .pdf), full resolution, black on transparent.

2. **Product 1:1 crop** — 1024×1024 PNG, transparent background, the figure centered for the 1:1 square panels used in the application chrome.

3. **Profile crop** — 4:5 portrait, 1024×1280 PNG, transparent background, used on filer profile pages.

4. **Editorial crop** — natural composition, no enforced aspect ratio, used on the marketing site where layout can flex.

5. **24×24 favicon-style icon** — heavily simplified silhouette that still reads as that filer. Test all four side by side at this size before delivering.

For the set:

- A single composite image of all four filers in a row, in roster order (01 Infiltrator, 02 Ghost, 03 Nexus, 04 Oracle), at full size and at 24px, for visual confirmation of consistency across the set.

## Working timeline (suggested)

1. **Round one — silhouette sketches.** Rough black-and-white sketches of all four at full size and 24px. Goal: confirm the compositions are distinct.

2. **Round two — refinement.** Final compositions, details locked, hand-paper economy clearly readable.

3. **Round three — crops and exports.** All deliverables packaged.
